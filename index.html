About

The European Set Theory Society aims to support research and networks in set theory and its connections with related areas in mathematics and logic. The scope of the society is worldwide.

Activities

The European Set Theory Society organises the biennial European Set Theory Conference series, at which the Hausdorff Medal is awarded. It announces important conferences and other events related to set theory and keeps track of worldwide hybrid and online activitites such as talks in the blog. It is affiliated with the European Mathematical Society. European set theory conference series


Set theory

Set theory is the mathematical theory of the infinite with numerous connections and applications in areas such as algebra, topology, real and complex analysis, functional analysis, measure theory, and theoretical computer science. Its origins lie in Georg Cantor's theory of transfinite numbers around 1874 and the axiomatization by Zermelo and others around 1908. Modern set theory began with the introduction of the method of forcing by Cohen in 1963 to study the continuum hypothesis. Set theory has witnessed a renaissance in the last decades with research directions and the creation of new research groups. History of set theory
